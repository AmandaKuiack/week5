A little bit about me:

My name is Amanda Kuiack, and I am in Set 1C of the CIT program here at BCIT. I have always been curious about computers, software, and video games; however, I am also very artistic. I love to draw, paint, work in photoshop, fold origami, and recently started casting jewelry and home decor in resin. Resin is actually really fun to work with, but also very dangerous due to the fumes. But it is cool to create a 3D piece of art that you can share with others. My parents were always certain I was going to be an artist, but growing up I wanted to work in the video game industry. I wanted to model characters and create textures and design levels. Unfortunately, I graduated during the 2008 recession. Without a job, I went back to school to change careers to get a job in the hospitality industry instead. I worked at a Spa chain in Vacouver for almost 8 years and became an Assistant Manager at one of their many locations. Thanks to the pandemic I was able to rethink my career after many months of feeling burnt out. I didn't want to return to Video Games, and after my dad had almost fallen victim to a software security scam, I decided I wanted to work towards a career in cybersecurity. So I upgrading my Math grades and applied for BCIT!
